A \*\*Z-fold\*\* simplifies the mechanical complexity by 90\\%. You're
essentially building a three-story aluminum sandwich.

By ditching the swivel and going for a straight 180\^\\circ flip on both
joints, you create a device that is a solid \"brick\" for your pocket
but unfolds into a vertical command center.

\### 1. The \"Z-Fold\" Mechanical Stack

Since you\'re looking for a \"rough but polished\" aluminum look,
you\'ll want \*\*Friction/Torque Hinges\*\*. These allow the screens to
stay exactly where you set them, so they don\'t flop around while
you\'re on your e-bike.

\* \*\*Layer 1 (Bottom):\*\* Aluminum box for the \*\*Rii K01V3\*\* guts
and battery.

\* \*\*Layer 2 (Middle):\*\* Aluminum tray for \*\*Screen #1\*\*.

\* \*\*Layer 3 (Top):\*\* Aluminum lid for \*\*Screen #2\*\*.

\### 2. Recommended Friction Hinges (The \"Simple\" Fix)

Instead of sourcing rare GPD parts, you can use industrial-grade
friction hinges. These are small, rugged, and bolt directly to your
aluminum boxes.

\* \*\*Southco E6 series:\*\* These are the gold standard for \"stiff\"
hinges that hold weight. They are black acetal or polished metal and
look very \"IT-Pro.\"

\* \*\*Link:\*\* Southco E6 Constant Torque Hinge (Look for the
180\^\\circ versions).

\* \*\*The \"Smithe\" Hack:\*\* Since you have big hands, you can use
the \*\*Adjustable Torque\*\* versions. You tighten a single screw to
make the hinge harder or easier to move---perfect for keeping the
screens stable against the vibrations of your bike.

\### 3. The \"Z\" Wiring Strategy

Since you're using the \*\*8.8\" DSI screens\*\*, your wiring is
actually much safer in a Z-fold:

\* \*\*Joint 1 (Base to Screen 1):\*\* One DSI ribbon cable.

\* \*\*Joint 2 (Screen 1 to Screen 2):\*\* The second DSI ribbon cable.

\* \*\*The Benefit:\*\* Because it\'s a Z-fold, you don\'t need any
complex \"slack loops\" for rotation. You just need a tiny bit of extra
length at the hinge so the ribbon can bend 180\^\\circ without snapping.

\### 4. \"Phone Mode\" Realism

With the Z-fold:

\* \*\*Closed:\*\* \*\*Screen #2\*\* is facing the world. It's your
\"Ticker Tape\" phone. You can see your AI node status or GPS without
opening a thing.

\* \*\*The \"N-Fold\" Move:\*\* If you want to use the keyboard and
\*one\* screen, you just unfold the first hinge.

\* \*\*The Full \"Nexus\":\*\* Unfold both for the dual-stacked 8.8\"
display.

\### Why this is \"Cooking with Bacon\":

By using a standard aluminum box and standard friction hinges, you\'re
building a \*\*Tank\*\*.

\* If a hinge gets loose, you tighten the screw.

\* If the aluminum gets scratched, you hit it with the Scotch-Brite pad.

\* The \*\*8.8\" width\*\* means the hinges can be wide and beefy---you
could probably drop this thing on the pavement in Knoxville and it would
just get a \"battle scar\" on the aluminum rather than shattering.
